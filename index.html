About Lord Venkateshwara

Lord Venkateshwara, also known as Balaji, Srinivasa, or Govinda, is one of the most revered incarnations of Lord Vishnu, who descended to Earth during the Kali Yuga.
He is the presiding deity of the Tirumala Venkateshwara Temple, a sacred site that draws millions of devotees seeking his blessings, love, and protection.

One of the most well-known stories about Lord Venkateshwara is his appearance on Earth. According to Hindu mythology, during the age of Kali Yuga, the world was plagued by sin and suffering.
Seeing the distress of the people, Lord Vishnu decided to descend to Earth to save humanity. He took the form of Venkateshwara and resided on the seven hills of Tirumala.

One of the most touching stories is about his marriage to Goddess Padmavati. Legend has it that Lord Venkateshwara fell in love with the princess Padmavati, an incarnation of Goddess Lakshmi.
However, to marry her, Venkateshwara had to take a large loan from Kubera, the god of wealth, to finance the grand wedding. It is believed that Lord Venkateshwara promised to repay this debt over time,
and even today, devotees offer donations at the Tirumala temple, contributing to the repayment of this divine loan.

This story symbolizes the selfless devotion of Lord Venkateshwara towards humanity, as he agreed to take on a debt to ensure the well-being and prosperity of his devotees.

The Power of His Grace

Lord Venkateshwara is also considered the deity of Kaliyuga, the current age according to Hindu belief. It is said that those who visit his temple and offer sincere prayers are blessed with wealth, health,
and happiness. His famous mantra, "Govinda! Govinda!", echoes through the hearts of his devotees, who chant it to feel his divine presence.

His temple at Tirumala is not only one of the wealthiest temples in the world, but it is also a symbol of faith, where miracles and blessings are experienced daily. The sight of the Lord, adorned in jewels and silks,
is a mesmerizing vision of divinity.

With his hand in a blessing pose (abhaya hasta), Lord Venkateshwara assures his devotees that he will always be there to protect and guide them.

"Yedukodal Vada Venkataramana Govinda Govinda!"
